STAR Story 1

Situation: Rebuilding the Network at a High School, in the process of transferring all of the crucial data from the old Domain Controller to the the new Physical Host for a virtual DC (An 18 hour transfer of data), the power to the building gets cut off by a construction team, with no warning. We are informed that the power cannot be brought back for at least 2 days while they carry out their required work.

Task: Assess the situation and create a plan to move forward. Determine if the power loss has caused any data loss. Determine how our schedule will be affected and if our work can still be complete on time.

Action: With no power on site, the hardware needed to be moved to one of our sites that did have power. Upon getting them to one of our other sites, the damage was assessed and large amounts of data was corrupted and lost, due to the power cutting it off mid transfer. So I first restored the most recent backup we had of the data to minimise the loss. Before comitting to the 18 hours of data transger, we first wanted to test it in the original environment, so we had to wait for the power to come back on at the original site. After 3 days, the power was returned, and we began testing the current version of the DC's backup. After determining that the loss was minimal-to-none, we restarted the data transfer. In all this particular task had put us 4 days behind schedule.

Result: Eventually the work on the DC was completed. Due to our whole process now being slightly behind schedule, we had to leave some low priority work to be completed during term time, such as reprogramming of switches in a currently unused block of classrooms. This meant our interference during term time was minimal and the transition was painless for staff.
